Economic & Financial Ties

Case Study: The Arkansas Teacher Retirement System (ATRS)

Executive Summary

This report delivers an irrefutable indictment of the Arkansas Teacher Retirement System's (ATRS) profound and systemic financial complicity in the Israeli colonial-apartheid project. Through its vast investment portfolio, ATRS channels the deferred wages of Arkansas educators into a global network of corporations and financial instruments that directly and indirectly finance, enable, and legitimize an apartheid regime and its ongoing, flagrant violations of international law and Palestinian human rights.

Across all major asset classes—Public Equities, Fixed Income, and the deliberately obscured Opaque Assets (Private Equity, Real Assets, Alternatives)—ATRS investments are systematically funneled through dominant asset managers such as BlackRock, Loomis Sayles, KKR, and Blackstone. These financial behemoths, in turn, direct capital into a web of companies deeply implicated in the Israeli military-industrial complex, the construction and maintenance of illegal colonial settlements, and the provision of advanced surveillance and population control technologies used to subjugate the Palestinian people.

ATRS's own "pecuniary factors only" investment policy... transforms ATRS from a neutral administrator of teacher pensions into an active financial underwriter of apartheid.

The pervasive opacity characterizing significant portions of ATRS's portfolio functions as a deliberate mechanism of concealment. This is not a mere administrative detail but an illegitimate veil of secrecy, shielding these financial ties from public scrutiny and democratic challenge. This report serves as a weapon for ideological struggle, providing clear, documented evidence to arm activists, ATRS members, and the public.

The Enablers: Policy and Politics

ATRS's "Pecuniary Factors Only" Policy

ATRS's investment policy, reinforced by Arkansas Act 411 of 2023, mandates a narrow, financially-driven approach. This framework provides a legal and ethical shield for maintaining investments in corporations perpetrating gross human rights violations. It institutionalizes a willful blindness to the profound human costs of investment decisions, granting a license for complicity.

The Arkansas Political Context

The state's reactionary political ecosystem, characterized by Republican supermajorities and deep corporate influence (Walmart, Stephens Inc., Tyson Foods), actively fosters this complicity. A legislature ideologically aligned with corporate interests and a conservative foreign policy is unlikely to support any serious investigation into ATRS's investments for human rights violations.

Unanswered Questions & Strategic Demands

To the ATRS Board: How do you justify concealing over $4.6 billion in "Collective Investment Trusts"?

"The ATRS mission speaks of 'Accountability and Accessibility' and 'Trust and Transparency.' How do you reconcile these stated values with the fact that over $4.6 billion of Arkansas teachers' retirement funds are concealed within opaque 'Collective Investment Trusts,' including those managed by State Street Global Advisors, with no public disclosure of the specific companies being invested in?"

To the ATRS Board: How is it in the 'best interest' of teachers to fund the killing of Palestinian children?

"ATRS claims to act in the 'best interest of ATRS members.' Many of your members are educators deeply committed to human rights. How is it in their 'best interest' for their deferred wages to be channeled into entities directly complicit in the brutalization, dispossession, and killing of Palestinian children, families, and educators?"

To Arkansas Legislators: How can you defend laws that mandate financial complicity in apartheid?

"As elected officials entrusted with oversight of public pension funds, how can you defend legislation like Act 411 of 2023, which compels the Arkansas Teacher Retirement System to prioritize narrowly defined 'pecuniary factors' over universally recognized human rights and international law? Does this not effectively mandate Arkansas teachers' financial complicity in apartheid and potential war crimes?"

Core Demands for Divestment

  • Immediate and Full Public Disclosure: Publish a complete, itemized list of ALL underlying holdings, especially within the opaque funds.
  • Adoption of Comprehensive Ethical Screens: Implement robust, transparent criteria to exclude all investments in companies complicit in violations of Palestinian human rights.
  • Time-Bound and Comprehensive Divestment: Commit to a clear, publicly announced plan for the complete divestment from all identified complicit assets.
  • Reinvestment in Just Futures: Actively seek to reinvest divested funds into ventures that promote peace, justice, and sustainable development.
